# APEX papers - Propus mapping

Read date: 2026-06-18.

APEX is an overloaded name. Propus keeps three relevant APEX ideas separate
instead of collapsing them into one generic "optimize the prompt" rule.

## Automated Prompt Engineering eXpert (arXiv:2606.11459)

Source: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11459>

Useful principle: prompt and skill optimization is often data-selection limited.
Lineage history can stratify cases into Easy, Hard, and Mixed. Mixed cases are
the frontier for ranking candidate quality and generating informative changes;
Easy cases remain anchors; Hard-only failures are dangerous mutation fuel.

Propus rule: prioritize Mixed frontier validation cases, retain Easy anchors,
and avoid Hard-only prompt or skill rewrites until the failure is addressable.

## Adaptive Principle EXtraction (arXiv:2606.15363)

Source: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15363>

Useful principle: Self-Harness is one axis, not the whole system. Production
self-evolution should distinguish harness patching, behavioral principle
distillation, and workflow topology selection over a shared trace pool.

Propus rule: classify each change by axis: harness patch, principle
distillation, or workflow topology. A prompt/skill patch alone is not proof that
the whole Propus system is complete.

## Autonomous Policy Exploration (arXiv:2605.21240)

Source: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21240>

Useful principle: self-evolving agents can collapse into familiar high-reward
routines. Maintaining an explicit map of tried routes and plausible unexplored
directions is the antidote.

Propus rule: treat the opportunity backlog as an exploration map. Record tried
routes, surface unexplored forks, and balance exploitation against frontier
discovery.
